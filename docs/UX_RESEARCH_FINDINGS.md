# VoicePay Africa UX Research Findings

## Ghana Mobile Payment Market
- **Market Size**: US$6.38bn projected for 2025 (25.35% growth)
- **Mobile Money Accounts**: 73 million registered, 22+ million active
- **Transaction Volume**: GHC 334.8 billion in 2024
- **Mobile Penetration**: 37 million subscribers for 31 million population

## Voice Command Patterns

### English Commands
- "Send [amount] to [contact name/number]"
- "Pay [merchant name] [amount]"
- "Check my balance"
- "Buy [amount] airtime for [number]"

### Twi Commands
- "Fa [amount] kɔma [contact]" (Send amount to contact)
- "Tua [merchant] [amount]" (Pay merchant amount)
- "Hwɛ me sika" (Check my money/balance)
- "Tɔ airtime" (Buy airtime)

### Hausa Commands
- "Aika [amount] zuwa [contact]" (Send amount to contact)
- "Biya [merchant] [amount]" (Pay merchant amount)
- "Duba kudin na" (Check my money)

## Critical UX Considerations
1. **Mobile-first**: 80% of Ghana uses mobile phones
2. **Low-bandwidth optimization**: 61% rural users experience weekly outages
3. **Multi-language support**: English, Twi, Hausa essential
4. **Visual fallbacks**: Always provide touch/tap alternatives
5. **Clear confirmations**: Verbal + visual transaction summaries
6. **Cultural sensitivity**: Natural financial terminology patterns

## Error Handling Messages
- **Voice Unclear**: "I couldn't understand that clearly. Please try again."
- **ENS Not Found**: "The ENS name you mentioned doesn't exist. Please check and try again."
- **Insufficient Funds**: "You don't have enough balance for this transaction."
- **Network Issues**: "Network is busy. Trying again in a moment..."
